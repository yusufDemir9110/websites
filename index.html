Turkish Submarine Fleet

The Submarine Fleet Command operates under the Turkish Naval Forces. Its duty is to manage the submarines in the inventory and to protect the homeland against any danger that may come from the submarine.

The fleet trains personnel, assigns them to submarines. Also watch the maintenance, repair and supply of submarines.

Turkish Submarine Fleet contains 12 submarines. 4 of Ay Class, 4 of Preveze Class, 4 of Gur Class...

Ay Class Submarines

The Ay Class is a submarine class that entered service in 1975 for the first of the Type 209 class submarines of Germany's Howaldtswerke Deutsche Werft AG company in the Turkish Naval Forces.

The names of the submarines, which are the ancestors of these six ships, were given by Ataturk, the founder of the Republic of Turkey. Ships can take the names of ships that were taken out of service years ago. So, these names were given to these submarines after the ancestors had decommissioned.

Since the names of all submarines end with "ay", this class was called the Ay. "Ay" means "Moon" in Turkish.

TCG BATIRAY (S-349)

She causes a lot of maintenance costs due to her old age. Because of this, she expected to be taken commissioned shortly

Preveze Class Submarines

The Preveze Class is a submarine class that entered service in 1994 for Turkish Naval Forces.

Thanks to these submarines, the Turkish navy introduced to guided missiles that can be fired from submarines.

Not only name of class but also first ship's name of class is Preveze. The Battle of Preveze was a naval battle that took place on 28 September 1538 near Preveza in Ionian Sea in northwestern Greece between an Ottoman fleet and that of a Holy League.

Displacement: 1400 tons
Length, Beam, Draught: 64m x 6.3m x 5.5m

22 kts. (Submerged)

TCG PREVEZE (S-353)

TCG Preveze,the first ship of Preveze Class, launched in November 1993 and commissioned in July 1994. She was built in Golcuk Shipyard.

She has joined Fost Trainings in United Kingdom. In 1997, she went to India and became the first submarine in the history of Turkish submarines to go to the Far East. She covered 14280 nautical miles while this cruise.

TCG SAKARYA (S-354)

TCG Sakarya, launched in July 1994 and commissioned in December 1995. She was built in Golcuk Shipyard.

She is the first Preveze Class submarine to participate in Fost Trainings in United Kingdom. Also she is the first Turkish submarine which visit Amsterdam, Netherlands.

Also, she is the first submarine which joined personnel rescue training with rescue chumber. The personel was transfered from TCG Sakarya at 135 meters depth.

TCG 18 MART (S-355)

TCG 18 Mart, launched in August 1997 and commissioned in July 1998. She was built in Golcuk Shipyard.

She has participated in many missions to date, including NATO and United Nations missions. She has joined Fost Trainings in United Kingdom.

The Tigerfish torpedo was fired first time by TCG 18 Mart.

TCG ANAFARTALAR (S-356)

TCG Anafartalar, launched in September 1998 and commissioned in July 1999. She was built in Golcuk Shipyard.

She has participated in many missions to date, including NATO and United Nations missions. She has joined Fost Trainings in United Kingdom.

Also, she is the first submarine which joined personnel rescue training with DSRV. The personel was transfered from TCG Anafartalar at 97 meters depth.

Gur Class Submarines

The Gur Class is a submarine class that entered service in 2003 for Turkish Naval Forces.

Gur class contains the most advanced submarines in the Turkish Navy in terms of weapons, communication and fire control systems.

Not only name of class but also first ship's name of class is Gur."Gur" means "Bushy,Rich,Strong" in Turkish.
